Conclusion

Personality:

Traditionally, female roles in TV dramas were often limited by societal norms and expectations. They were always described as nurturing figures, emotional, or secondary characters whose primary function was to support the male lead. In contrast, online dramas have changed female personalities into diverse and complex. Today, we see women depicted as multidimensional roles with independency, ambitions, and having self-conscious. They are leaders, rebels, visionaries, and more, each with a unique voice.

Narrative:

The storyline of female characters has also significantly evolved. In traditional TV dramas, women's stories often revolved around family and romantic relationships, with limited exploration of other aspects of their lives. In the digital age, online dramas have expanded these narratives, placing women at the center of diverse storylines that explore career ambitions, personal growth. This shift allows for richer, more engaging storytelling that resonates with audiences seeking authenticity and relatability.

Conclusion:

The evolution of female characters from traditional TV dramas to online dramas in the digital age faces a significant cultural shift. It reflects the growing demand for true, diverse, and empowering representations of women on screen. This transformation has been influenced by societal progress towards gender equality, the rising of the feminist movement in the digital age. As we look to the future, it is likely that this trend will continue, with female characters becoming even more diverse and inspiring change both in media and society.
